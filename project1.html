A. P . J. Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man of India

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam born on October 15, 1931, was a renowned Indian scientist and the 11th President of India. He is widely celebrated as the "Missile Man of India" for his pivotal role in the development India's missile and nuclear programs Beyond his scientific achievements Kalam was a beloved figure known for his simple lifestyleinspiring leadership, and dedication to education and youth empowerment Kalam's career was marked by significant contributions to India's space and defense sectors He played a crucial role in the development of India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) and various missiles like Agni and Prithvi. He served as the Chief Executive of the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme and later as the Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation. His leadership was instrumental in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests, solidifying India's position as a nuclear power.
